How to onboard a physician


Onboarding a Physician

Information reqiured to onboard a physician to an existing group

    Below listed the items you may need to obtain inorder to begin the credentialing process for an individual provider to add to an existing / new group.

  1. Resume / CV
  2. CMS I & A Logins for Medicare Enrollment application
  3. CAQH Logins
  4. Medical License Copy
  5. Board / Specialty Certification
  6. DEA - If applicable
  7. Collaborating Physician Agreement - Incase of Midlevel Practitioner (NP, PA)
  8. Hospital Affliation / Admitting Priviliages (Required for PCP)
  9. Proffessional Liability Insurance (Either under the group / Individual)
  10. SSN, DOB, Home Address & Place of birthe (Country & City)
  11. Provider Role (PCP / Specialist)
  12. Adverse action / Malpractice report (If applicable)

Onboarding Solo-Practice

Onboarding Solo-Practice


    Solo practice is described by its namea practice without partners or employment affiliations with other practice organizations. Solo practices are usually characterized by a small staff and typically have a limited patient base. This smaller size and the autonomy of being the only physician gives the advantage of being able to design, grow, and develop the practice as you'd like relative to other practice settings.


    Information reqiured to onboard a Solo Practitioner:

    Solo Practitioners are those who work independently at their home office or in a office setting without additional provider collaborations. Providers can utilize their SSN for enrollment and billing purpose when they do solo practising. The restriction would be if they want to add a new provider from a different specialy then at that poit he/she would need update / amend the existing contracts & enrollments. In addition to the above list, you may need the below items to credential this type of provider.

  1. IRS Letter (CP575 /147C / W9 with SSN)
  2. Practice Address

    3. Headsup: Submitting Medicare application for Solo Practitioner is slighly different, you may need to check the options available in the PECOS.
